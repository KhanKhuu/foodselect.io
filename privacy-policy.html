Service Providers

I use a Google Cloud Service called Cloud Firestore to store data about ingredients. When you use your camera to scan ingredients labels or barcodes, or search for an ingredient, and when you open the app for the first time, your device communicates with Google's servers to pull information about ingredients. Their policy states "Cloud Functions uses IP addresses to execute event-handling functions and HTTP functions based on end-user actions. Cloud functions only saves IP addresses temporarily, to provide the service." The service's privacy policy can be accessed in full at Privacy and Security in Firebase
